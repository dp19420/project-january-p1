Summary

Synopsis

Narmer, also known as Menes, was the first pharaoh of unified Egypt, reigning around 3150 BCE during the Early Dynastic Period. He is credited with uniting Upper and Lower Egypt into a single kingdom, marking the beginning of ancient Egyptian civilization


Unification

Narmer conquered Lower Egypt and unified the country, creating the First Dynasty. This achievement is depicted on the famous Narmer Palette, which shows him wearing the crowns of both Upper and Lower Egypt


Capital and Trade

He founded Memphis, the first capital of unified Egypt, and developed significant trade with neighboring regions, including southern Canaan
